About Me

After spending 20 years in the music business I learned some things that will always be with me in my professional and personal life.
-Work hard and enjoy whatever you do.
-Play well with others.
-Inspire and be inspired.
These principles carry with me in my new career as a web developer. This is a new field for me but it really isn’t different than my previous career, only the syntax is different. You see, as a composer and producer you start from the same place, a blank slate. With a blank slate you start to fill it with ideas, good or bad (a melody or page layout). Edit and expand those ideas (alter a chord structure or page layout). Continue building the structure (add a guitar or a function). Start to build a team around those ideas (hire a guitar player or consult with the back end engineer). Continue filling those ideas out (add piano or bring in the art team). Continue these steps, getting feedback along the way, working with the team, until you reach a product that’s ready for the world to hear or see. This is a bit of a simplification but it should illustrate the parallels between the two disciplines. This is why when I decided to change careers coding seemed like a natural fit.
